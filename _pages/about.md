Hi there! I'm Haoyang (William) Wu, a junior student majoring in Computer Science at the University of Michigan. I'm also pursuing a dual degree in Mechanical Engineering at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), with an expected graduation in 2027.

My research focuses on endowing embodied AI with the robust generalization and reasoning skills inherent in scalable Large/Vision Language Models. I aim to develop scalable frameworks that enable robots to translate high-level semantic understanding into precise, adaptive manipulation in unseen settings. I am currently a research intern at Princeton University, working with [Prof. Zhuang Liu](https://liuzhuang13.github.io) and [Prof. Danqi Chen](https://www.cs.princeton.edu/~danqic/). I also work as a research intern in the [ARM Lab](https://arm.robotics.umich.edu) directed by [Prof. Dmitry Berenson](https://berenson.robotics.umich.edu). Previously at SJTU, I was deeply engaged in surgical robotics research at the [SIRIUS Lab](https://banyutong.github.io/sirius_lab_website/), mentored by [Prof. Yutong Ban](https://people.csail.mit.edu/yban/). You can find my resume (updated 04/06/2025) [here](https://william-hywu.github.io/files/Resume_Haoyang Wu_internship.pdf)

**I will be joining Princeton University as a visiting intern this summer! See you in Princeton**
